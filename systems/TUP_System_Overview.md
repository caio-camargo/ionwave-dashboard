## TUP STAGES

### Stage 1: Idea
**Status:** Concept identified  
**Work:** Thesis drafted  
**Timeline:** Days to weeks  
**Gate:** Worth researching?

### Stage 2: Research
**Status:** Active investigation  
**Work:** Market research, competitive analysis  
**Timeline:** 2-4 weeks  
**Gate:** Worth detailed planning?

### Stage 3: Planning (Imagination Sprint)
**Status:** Full business plan development  
**Work:** Complete Imagination Sprint deliverables  
**Timeline:** 21 days  
**Gate:** Imagination Gate (GO/NO-GO)

### Stage 4: Approved
**Status:** Passed Imagination Gate, funded  
**Work:** Implementation Sprint preparation  
**Timeline:** 1-2 weeks  
**Gate:** Ready for Implementation?

### Stage 5: Implementation
**Status:** Building assets, preparing launch  
**Work:** Implementation Sprint  
**Timeline:** 30-45 days  
**Gate:** Implementation Gate (ready to launch?)

### Stage 6: Launched â†’ In-Process
**Status:** Live, generating revenue  
**Work:** Operations, optimization, scaling  
**Timeline:** Ongoing  
**Exits TUP, enters Operations tracking**

## TUP KILL CRITERIA

**When to kill a TUP (at each gate):**

### After Idea:
- Market too small (<$100M)
- Competition too intense (no gap)
- Can't find Operator fit

### After Research:
- W Formula score <50
- No clear PMF path
- Competitive moat weak

### After Planning:
- Unit economics don't work
- Can't validate customer need
- Timeline too long (>6 months to launch)

### After Implementation:
- Can't find product-market fit
- CAC too high (>LTV/3)
- Operator wants out

**Kill fast, kill often:** Most TUPs should die before launch

## TUP METRICS

### Pipeline Health:
- TUPs in each stage
- Conversion rate between stages
- Time in each stage
- Kill rate by stage

### Portfolio Balance:
- Mix of stages (don't have all in one stage)
- Operator capacity
- Capital deployed
- Risk diversification

### Success Metrics:
- % that reach launch
- % that reach $100K MRR
- % that become profitable
- Capital efficiency ($ invested / $ returned)
