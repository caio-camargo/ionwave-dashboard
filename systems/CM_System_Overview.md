# CM System Overview

**CM = Compliance Manager**

**Purpose:** Quality control for Trade execution  
**Owner:** Studio 3 System  
**Current CM:** Anya

---

## WHAT IS CM

The Compliance Manager is Studio 3's quality control function. CM:
- **Monitors** Trade execution against standards
- **Grades** deliverable quality
- **Alerts** when performance degrades
- **Escalates** critical issues
- **Gates** major phase transitions

**Think:** Quality inspector + early warning system

---

## CM RESPONSIBILITIES

### 1. Deliverable Grading
- Review each ODD deliverable
- Grade A-F based on rubric
- Provide specific feedback
- Track grade history

### 2. Performance Monitoring
- Track KPIs (quality, timeline, communication)
- Identify concerning trends
- Compare to benchmarks
- Assess overall Trade health

### 3. Alert Management
- Issue alerts when standards not met
- Define required corrective actions
- Track operator response
- Escalate if unresolved

### 4. Gate Decisions
- Evaluate readiness for phase transitions
- Recommend GO/NO-GO
- Document decision rationale
- Ensure criteria met before advancing

### 5. Post-Mortems
- Analyze Trade outcomes (success or failure)
- Extract learnings
- Update standards/rubrics
- Share insights across Trades

---

## CM GRADING SCALE

**A (90-100):** Exemplary
- Exceeds all standards
- Use as reference model
- Publishable quality

**B (80-89):** Acceptable
- Meets all standards
- Minor improvements suggested
- Ready to proceed

**C (70-79):** Below Standard
- Gaps in key areas
- Revision required
- Not ready to proceed

**D (60-69):** Poor
- Major deficiencies
- Significant rework needed
- Escalation required

**F (<60):** Unacceptable
- Fundamental failures
- May indicate Trade should be killed
- Immediate escalation

## CM DATA SOURCES

**CM monitors:**
- Deliverable submissions
- Progress reports
- Communication frequency
- Timeline adherence
- Quality trends
- Operator responsiveness

**CM does NOT:**
- Make strategic decisions (Operator/Founder role)
- Execute Trade work (Operator role)
- Manage day-to-day operations (Operator role)

**CM advises, monitors, and gates - but doesn't operate.**

---

## CM ASSESSMENT CADENCE

**Daily:**
- Monitor active alerts
- Review responses
- Check critical metrics

**Weekly:**
- Grade new deliverables
- Update Trade status
- Identify new issues
- Communicate with operators

**Monthly:**
- Comprehensive Trade review
- Update overall grades
- Post-mortem on completed Trades
- Refine standards based on learnings

**Quarterly:**
- Portfolio health assessment
- CM system improvements
- Benchmark updates

---

## CM INDEPENDENCE

**CM operates independently:**
- Not subordinate to Operator
- Reports to Founder
- Can downgrade or block progress
- Cannot be overruled by Operator

**Why:** Quality control requires independence
