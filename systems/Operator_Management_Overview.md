# Operator Management Overview

**Purpose:** How Studio 3 finds, evaluates, onboards, and supports Operators  
**Applies To:** All Trade Operators  
**Owner:** GM (Caio)

---

## WHAT IS AN OPERATOR

**Operator = CEO of their specific Trade**

**Responsibilities:**
- Execute Trade from planning through operations
- Make day-to-day decisions
- Build and manage team
- Hit milestones
- Report progress

**Compensation:**
- Equity in their Trade
- Upside tied to Trade success
- No guaranteed salary (risk/reward)

---

## OPERATOR LIFECYCLE

### 1. Sourcing
**Where we find Operators:**
- Referrals (best source)
- LinkedIn outreach
- Previous Studio 3 participants
- Entrepreneurial communities
- Business school alumni

### 2. Screening
**Initial filter:**
- Review resume/background
- Check for relevant experience
- Assess cultural fit indicators
- Phone screen (30 min)

### 3. Evaluation
**Deep assessment:**
- Focused interviews
- Case study/simulation
- Reference checks
- TUP assignment fit

### 4. Selection
**Final decision:**
- Scorecard review
- Team discussion
- Offer extended
- Contract negotiation

### 5. Onboarding
**First 30 days:**
- Studio 3 orientation
- Systems training
- TUP handoff
- Support setup

### 6. Support
**Ongoing:**
- Weekly GM check-ins
- CM assessment feedback
- Blocker removal
- Coaching as needed

### 7. Graduation
**Trade transitions:**
- From Imagination → Implementation
- From Implementation → Operations
- From Operations → Exit/Scale
- Operator may transition to next Trade

## OPERATOR SCORECARD

**Evaluate candidates on:**

### Must-Haves (Deal Breakers):
- [ ] Entrepreneurial mindset
- [ ] Execution capability (ships things)
- [ ] Resourcefulness (figures it out)
- [ ] Integrity (trustworthy)
- [ ] Culture fit (Studio 3 values)

### Strong Preferences:
- [ ] DTC experience
- [ ] Marketing/growth skills
- [ ] Previous startup experience
- [ ] Relevant domain expertise
- [ ] Data-driven approach

### Nice-to-Haves:
- [ ] Technical skills
- [ ] Design sense
- [ ] Previous founder
- [ ] Studio 3 alumni

**Scoring:** Each dimension rated 1-5, must hit minimum thresholds

## OPERATOR SOURCING STRATEGY

### Channels:

**Tier 1 (Best):**
- Referrals from portfolio
- Previous Studio 3 participants
- Known entrepreneurial operators

**Tier 2 (Good):**
- LinkedIn targeted outreach
- Entrepreneurial communities
- Business school networks

**Tier 3 (Volume):**
- Job postings
- Recruiting firms
- General applications

**Conversion rates:**
- Tier 1: 20-30% (known quantities)
- Tier 2: 5-10% (qualified leads)
- Tier 3: 1-2% (volume play)

## OPERATOR OUTREACH

**Messaging for recruitment:**

**Hook:** Build a business with experienced team + capital

**Pitch:**
- You get: Equity in Trade you build
- You don't need: Your own capital, solo grind
- We provide: Systems, support, capital, team
- You provide: Execution, leadership, drive

**Call-to-action:** 30-min exploratory call

## OPERATOR SUCCESS METRICS

**How we evaluate Operators:**

### Execution Metrics:
- Milestone hit rate
- Timeline adherence
- Quality of deliverables
- Speed of iteration

### Results Metrics:
- Trade performance (revenue, growth)
- Unit economics achievement
- Team building
- Capital efficiency

### Process Metrics:
- Documentation quality
- Communication frequency
- Problem identification
- Risk management

**Grade:** CM provides ongoing assessment

---

## OPERATOR OFFBOARDING

**When Operator transitions out:**

### Scenarios:
- **Success:** Trade graduates, Operator moves to next or exits
- **Pivot:** Trade killed, Operator may get new TUP
- **Performance:** Not working out, parting ways

### Process:
- [ ] Transition plan created
- [ ] Knowledge transfer
- [ ] Handoff to next Operator (if applicable)
- [ ] Equity treatment per contract
- [ ] Exit interview
- [ ] Alumni status (if positive)
